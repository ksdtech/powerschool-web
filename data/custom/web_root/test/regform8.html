Medical Information

9a. Medication - Daily and 72 hour Supply
In order to administer prescription or over-the-counter medication at school, the District requires a medication form, signed by a doctor. The forms for daily medication ("Authorization to Administer Medication"; a version is also available in Spanish) and for 72 hour emergency medication ("72 Hour Medication Form for Emergency Preparedness"; or version in Spanish) can be downloaded by clicking on the links above, or picked up in one of the school offices. More information is available from the Marin County Office of Education Medications page.

Please complete all appropriate form(s) and return them to the school office. You must bring the required medication in the original container, labeled with the student's name, on the first day of school or as soon as new medication is dispensed. If you need additional help, please contact the District nurse, Becky Gayman, at bgayman@marin.k12.ca.us
