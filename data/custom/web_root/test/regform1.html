Fifth Grade Music Program
All fifth graders will be enrolled in Beginning Band.
The first year of instrumental music focuses on learning to read music and basic ensemble skills. Students will also be taught individual practice skills and are expected to practice a minimum of sixty minutes per week. Students may choose from the following instruments: flute, clarinet, trumpet, trombone or percussion. Students who wish to play the saxophone in the sixth grade band must start on clarinet for the first year for pedagogical reasons. Students do not need to decide which instrument they want to play until after classes have started; information about instrument rentals will be sent home with students during the first week of school.
Upon completion of Beginning Band, sixth-grade students will have the opportunity to continue in the instrumental music program or begin singing in the choral program. Please feel free to email Mr. Martinez at mmartinez@kentfieldschools.org for more information.
Required and Optional Sixth Grade Performing Arts Selections
All sixth graders must select a performing arts class (either Intermediate Band or Intermediate Chorus) that occurs during the regular school day.
Sixth Grade Performing Arts: Intermediate Band
The prerequisite for intermediate band is completion of Beginning Band or by audition. The second year of instrumental music improves upon the musical and ensemble skills learned in Beginning Band and adds multi-part sheet music, syncopation, and major and minor scales. A minimum of 100 minutes practice per week is required.
Sixth Grade Performing Arts: Intermediate Chorus
Students who choose to participate in intermediate chorus will learn musical and vocal techniques through interval training, scale studies and part singing. Song selections will range from popular to classical. A minimum of 100 minutes practice per week is required.
Please note: enrollment in any band or choral ensemble is a full-year commitment; if you have difficulty or have questions in selecting a music program for your student, please contact Mr. Martinez at mmartinez@kentfieldschools.org (preferred) or at (415) 257-0733, ext. 13 #.

Seventh and Eighth Grade Music Program
Zero-Period Advanced Band
The concert band meets during zero period, which begins at 7:45 A.M. on Monday, Wednesday, and Friday of each week. Concert band students must have had two to three years of previous playing experience. Emphasis is placed on gaining musical independence through more extensive scale studies, technical exercises, and more advanced sheet music. Students work on music from a wide range of musical styles, including jazz and classical. A minimum of 100 minutes practice per week is required.
Zero-Period Concert Choir
All sixth, seventh, and eighth grade students are eligible to participate in concert choir which meets during zero period at 7:45 A.M. on Tuesday and Thursday of each week. Continued emphasis is placed on reading music as well as two, three, and even four part a cappella as well as accompanied singing.
Both the advanced band and concert choir perform at least twice during the school year.
Please note: enrollment in any band or choral ensemble is a full-year commitment; if you have difficulty or have questions in selecting a music program for your student, please contact Mr. Martinez at mmartinez@kentfieldschools.org (preferred) or at (415) 257-0733, ext. 13 #.

Eighth Grade Enrichment Classes
Eighth grade students will be enrolled in enrichment classes each trimester. Every eighth-grade student will take a required trimester class of writing workshop; as well, students will be enrolled in two different enrichment classes selected from the following list:
  • Architecture
  • Art
  • Technology
  • Video
  • Wood Shop
Eighth Grade Architecture
Design a residence, a 3-D virtual museum on computer software or explore the world of sustainable, green design. In this course, students will design a project that realizes their own interests by bringing them to life. Students will have the choice to use computer software to enhance their design or to build a scale model of their project. Examples of past projects have included a unique sushi restaurant with an interior waterway, a detailed interior design of a residence, a fashion runway, a museum, and a skateboard park. This course will be offered one trimester with an enrollment limit of 20 students.
Eighth Grade Art
Eighth grade students will do a series of art projects beginning with a color review and a value study. Other projects that students may work on in this studio include linoleum block carving and printing, sculpture, and a combination painting and performance piece involving art history. This course is offered for 20 students each trimester.
Eighth Grade Technology
Students in this technology lab will produce and maintain Kent's student web pages (www.falconnews.org). Students create and produce the Monday morning Falcon News broadcast. Participants will develop skills in web design: organization of visual, audio, and content elements to attract visitors; and in basic TV journalism: researching, writing, and producing Kent-relevant news stories. This course is offered for 20 students each trimester.
Eighth Grade Video
This video boot camp will expose students to the fundamental elements of visual storytelling: camera, sound, lighting and editing. Students will work in small production crews to complete practice assignments that demonstrate their understanding of each of these elements, and finally will produce a short video project. This is a hands-on class where students will use video cameras, sound equipment, and computer editing programs. Collaboration, creativity, and responsibility are emphasized. This course is offered for 20 students each trimester.
Eighth Grade Wood Shop
From a choice of hardwoods, design and build that heirloom project for yourself or your family; one that will be passed on from generation to generation. Students will learn advanced techniques using woodworking machinery to build their masterpieces. Previous projects have included musical instruments, furniture, skateboards and advanced lathe work; many of these projects have won best of show at the Marin County Fair and ribbons at the California State Fair. This course is offered for 20 students for two trimesters.
