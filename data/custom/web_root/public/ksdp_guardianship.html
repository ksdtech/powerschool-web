Legal Guardianship
Legal guardianship is a court order that says someone who is not the child’s parent is in charge of taking care of the child. Legal guardians have a lot of the same rights and responsibilities as parents. They can decide where the child lives and goes to school, and they can make decisions about the child’s health care.
There are 2 kinds of guardianships in California. Most guardianship cases are in probate court. These cases are brought by the person seeking to be appointed guardian or by someone else in the family asking the court to appoint a guardian.
Another kind of guardianship arises when the custody of a minor is awarded to a non-parent through the juvenile dependency court.
