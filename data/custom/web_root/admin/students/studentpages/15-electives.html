Kent School Music Programs: Descriptions
Fifth Grade Music Program
All fifth graders will be enrolled in Beginning Band and other enrichment classes, but there are no enrichment choices to be made. Please click the Submit button below to continue.
You may email Mr. Martinez at mmartinez@kentfieldschools.org for more information about the music program at Kent.
Required Sixth Grade Performing Arts Selection
All sixth graders must select a performing arts class (either Intermediate Band or Intermediate Chorus) that occurs during the regular school day.
Sixth Grade Performing Arts: Intermediate Band
The prerequisite for intermediate band is completion of Beginning Band or by audition. The second year of instrumental music improves upon the musical and ensemble skills learned in Beginning Band and adds multi-part sheet music, syncopation, and major and minor scales.
Sixth Grade Performing Arts: Intermediate Chorus
Students who choose to participate in intermediate chorus will learn musical and vocal techniques through interval training, scale studies and part singing. Song selections will range from popular to classical.
Please note: enrollment in any band or choral ensemble is a full-year commitment; if you have difficulty or have questions in selecting a music program for your student, please contact Mr. Martinez at mmartinez@kentfieldschools.org (preferred) or at (415) 257-0733, ext. 13 #.

Seventh and Eighth Grades: Zero-Period Music Program
Zero-Period Advanced Band
The concert band meets during zero period, which begins at 7:45 A.M. on Monday, Wednesday, and Friday of each week. Concert band students must have had two to three years of previous playing experience. Emphasis is placed on gaining musical independence through more extensive scale studies, technical exercises, and more advanced sheet music. Students work on music from a wide range of musical styles, including jazz and classical.
Zero-Period Concert Choir
All sixth, seventh, and eighth grade students are eligible to participate in concert choir which meets during zero period at 7:45 A.M. on Tuesday and Thursday of each week. Continued emphasis is placed on reading music as well as two, three, and even four part a cappella as well as accompanied singing.
Both the advanced band and concert choir perform at least twice during the school year.
Please note: enrollment in any band or choral ensemble is a full-year commitment; if you have difficulty or have questions in selecting a music program for your student, please contact Mr. Martinez at mmartinez@kentfieldschools.org (preferred) or at (415) 257-0733, ext. 13 #.
