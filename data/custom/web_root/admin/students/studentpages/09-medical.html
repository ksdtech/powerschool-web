Medical Information
Important Note:: Any medical information changes must be completed here and also emailed directly to the District nurse, Becky Gayman, at bgayman@kentfieldschools.org
1. Medication - Daily and 72 hour Supply
In order to administer prescription or over-the-counter medication at school, the District requires a medication form, signed by a doctor. The forms for daily medication ("Authorization to Administer Medication"; a version is also available in Spanish) and for 72 hour emergency medication ("72 Hour Medication Form for Emergency Preparedness"; or version in Spanish) can be downloaded by clicking on the links above, or picked up in one of the school offices. More information is available from the Marin County Office of Education School Health Manual page.

Please complete all appropriate form(s) and return them to the school office. You must bring the required medication in the original container, labeled with the student's name, on the first day of school or as soon as new medication is dispensed. If you need additional help, please contact the District nurse, Becky Gayman, at bgayman@kentfieldschools.org

2. Health Insurance and Providers
Important Notice: The Children's Health Initiative of Marin County (Marin CHI) can assist families in applying for many options for free and low-cost health insurance for children from birth to age 19. Health plan options available include:
  • Healthy Kids
  • Kaiser Child Health Plan
  • Healthy Families
  • Medi-Cal
  • AIM
  • CalFresh
For more information, please call (415) 473-3434, or download the CHI Health Insurance flyer from the District's Health and Safety page.
