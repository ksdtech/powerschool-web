2b. Music and Enrichment Options - Grades 5-8 Only
You do not need to fill out this form for students who will be attending Bacich. There are no enrichment choices to be made.
Fifth Grade Music Program
All fifth graders will be enrolled in Beginning Band and other enrichment classes. There are no enrichment choices to be made. Please feel free to email Mr. Martinez at mmartinez@kentfieldschools.org for more information about the music program at Kent.
Required Sixth Grade Performing Arts Selection
All sixth graders must select a performing arts class (either Intermediate Band or Intermediate Chorus) that occurs during the regular school day.
Sixth Grade Performing Arts: Intermediate Band
The prerequisite for intermediate band is completion of Beginning Band or by audition. The second year of instrumental music improves upon the musical and ensemble skills learned in Beginning Band and adds multi-part sheet music, syncopation, and major and minor scales.
Sixth Grade Performing Arts: Intermediate Chorus
Students who choose to participate in intermediate chorus will learn musical and vocal techniques through interval training, scale studies and part singing. Song selections will range from popular to classical.
Please note: enrollment in any band or choral ensemble is a full-year commitment; if you have difficulty or have questions in selecting a music program for your student, please contact Mr. Martinez at mmartinez@kentfieldschools.org (preferred) or at (415) 257-0733, ext. 13 #.

Seventh and Eighth Grades: Music Program and Other Zero-Period Options
Zero-Period Advanced Band
The concert band meets during zero period, which begins at 7:45 A.M. on Monday, Wednesday, and Friday of each week. Concert band students must have had two to three years of previous playing experience. Emphasis is placed on gaining musical independence through more extensive scale studies, technical exercises, and more advanced sheet music. Students work on music from a wide range of musical styles, including jazz and classical.
Zero-Period Concert Choir
All sixth, seventh, and eighth grade students are eligible to participate in concert choir which meets during zero period at 7:45 A.M. on Tuesday and Thursday of each week. Continued emphasis is placed on reading music as well as two, three, and even four part a cappella as well as accompanied singing.
Both the advanced band and concert choir perform at least twice during the school year.
Please note: enrollment in any band or choral ensemble is a full-year commitment; if you have difficulty or have questions in selecting a music program for your student, please contact Mr. Martinez at mmartinez@kentfieldschools.org (preferred) or at (415) 257-0733, ext. 13 #.
Zero-Period Mathletes
Mathletes is a supportive group of 7th and 8th grade students who will compete in mathematical problem-solving competitions against teams from other middle schools in Marin County. At Kent, our goal is to offer Mathletes during zero period in order to give students further opportunities to work on fun and creative problems that promote critical-thinking and problem-solving skills as well as to extend the concepts that are learned in the students' math classes.
The Kent Mathletes Team will meet one to two mornings a week on Tuesdays and/or Thursdays from October till Mid-March (students will be excused from Chorus if attending Mathletes). The teams will learn strategies that help in solving math problems similar to the ones given in the Mathletes competitions. There are a total of four countywide competitions that occur after school in November, December, February, and March. In joining Mathletes students will be expected to participate in the competitions and make a commitment to attend the morning classes regularly.
Zero-Period Yearbook Committee
The yearbook committee will be put into place during zero period when the book is under production. It will meet one morning a week probably on Thursdays (students will be excused from Chorus if attending Yearbook). Key characteristics of any Yearbook Committee member will be reliability and humility. This opportunity is a commitment to attend the morning classes regularly when the book is under construction, and members will be required to sacrifice a few lunchtimes a month till the yearbook is sent out to be printed should it be necessary. Many yearbook alumnus go on to work on high school yearbooks, on high school newspapers such as The Redwood Bark, and in other clubs and committees. Yearbook is hard but rewarding work; students take great pride and satisfaction in contributing to the final product that becomes the Kent Annual.

Eighth Grade Enrichment Classes
Eighth grade students will be enrolled in trimester-long enrichment classes. Every eighth-grade student will take a required trimester class of Expressions; as well, students will be enrolled in two different enrichment classes selected from the following list:
  • Architecture
  • Art
  • Journalism
  • Video
  • Wood Shop
Eighth Grade Architecture
Design a residence, a 3-D virtual museum on computer software or explore the world of sustainable, green design. In this course, students will design a project that realizes their own interests by bringing them to life. Students will have the choice to use computer software to enhance their design or to build a scale model of their project. Examples of past projects have included a unique sushi restaurant with an interior waterway, a detailed interior design of a residence, a fashion runway, a museum, and a skateboard park.
Eighth Grade Art
In this class students extend their understanding of the Elements and Principles of Design learned in their fifth and sixth grade experiences. Through projects in drawing, printmaking, and design, the student artists employ the fundamental elements of line, shape, value, texture, color and form to explore the interrelationship of these ideas with principles such as balance, repetition, variation, emphasis and unity. They may use ink, graphite, charcoal, chalk/oil pastel, prismacolor, acrylic and watercolor.
In addition to their regular curriculum, students have studio workshops on block days. These workshops are process oriented designed to further enhance their development as artists and their understanding of techniques and materials.
Eighth Grade Journalism
Students in this technology-rich class will track down the latest Kent news stories, report them in best journalistic style in our online newspaper, film and edit videos of the Kent stories, and present the videos along with the bulletin in our morning broadcast. Collaboration, writing style, technical skills, and organization are emphasized.
Eighth Grade Video
Video Boot Camp is an advanced enrichment where students will learn the fundamental techniques of movie making from start to finish. This hands on class teaches visual story telling using camera, lighting, sound, and editing techniques. Students will create a short video production at the end of the trimester. Creativity, resourcefulness, and responsibility are emphasized.
Eighth Grade Wood Shop
From a choice of hardwoods, design and build that heirloom project for yourself or your family; one that will be passed on from generation to generation. Students will learn advanced techniques using woodworking machinery to build their masterpieces. Previous projects have included musical instruments, furniture, skateboards and advanced lathe work; many of these projects have won best of show at the Marin County Fair and ribbons at the California State Fair.
