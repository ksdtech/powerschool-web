General Information
Scheduling a student's day is a complicated process, and it is very important that you select a music class that is appropriate for your student. Students should choose carefully in making their music selection, as we are creating sections and staffing them according to student requests. Future changes will not be allowed, so please be sure to get you child's input before selecting!

Music enrollment is a full-year commitment; if you have difficulty in selecting a music program for your student, please contact the music teacher at (415) 257-0733, ext. 13#
Fifth Grade Music Program
All fifth graders will take either chorus or beginning band. The first year of instrumental music is spent learning to read music and to become a "solid" beginner on the flute, clarinet, trumpet, trombone, or percussion instrument. Students do not have to decide which instrument they want to play until after classes have started. Information about instrument choices will be sent home with students the first week of school. Band students are expected to practice at home. Instruments may be rented or purchased through local music stores. Financial assistance is available upon request.

Students choosing to participate in chorus will learn vocal techniques through unison and singing in harmony. Song selections will range from popular to classical. Chorus students also learn to play the recorder and read music.

Both the chorus and band students perform during the school year.

Sixth Grade Music Program
Sixth graders who are eligible to take intermediate band, will meet during the school day. Eligibility for intermediate band is by the music teacher's recommendation. Sixth grade students are also able to participate in concert choir which meets during zero period at 7:45 A.M. The concert choir will rehearse on Tuesday and Thursday mornings.

Seventh and Eighth Grade Music Program
The seventh and eighth grade concert band and concert choir will meet during zero period which begins at 7:45 A.M. The concert band meets on Monday, Wednesday, and Friday mornings while the concert choir meets on Tuesday and Thursday mornings.

Concert band students must have had two to three years of previous playing experience. Emphasis is placed on gaining musical independence through scale studies, technical exercises and improvisation. Students work on music from a wide range of musical styles, including jazz and classical. They perform concerts during the year.

Concert choir will meet twice per week. This is an advanced choral group that works on challenging a cappella material (without accompaniment). Students wishing to join must be able to match a pitch, sing in parts, and have the interest and enthusiasm that is required to develop a high-level choral ensemble. The group will work on music ranging from jazz to classical. Students with no previous choral experience, or who are new to the school, must audition with the music teacher. The choir will perform concerts during the year.

Eighth Grade Enrichment Classes
Enrichment classes will be offered to eighth grade students in three trimesters. Every 8th grade student will take a required trimester class in writing workshop. In each of the other two trimesters, every eighth grade student will take a different enrichment class, selected from the following list:
  • Art
  • Technology
  • Video
  • Wood Shop
This means that eighth graders will be taking classes in two of these four enrichment classes.

Please select your student's choices in their order of preference using the drop-down menus below. Because of the intricacies of scheduling, we will try to make sure that your student will get at least one of his/her top two choices.

If you have any questions about the eighth grade enrichment classes, please email Ms. Osterman. Her email address is losterman@kentfieldschools.org
