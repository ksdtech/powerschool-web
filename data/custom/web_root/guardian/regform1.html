Selecting Your Fifth Grade Music Class
Scheduling a student's day is a complicated process, and it is very important that you select a music class that is appropriate for your student. Students should choose carefully in making their music selection, as we are creating sections and staffing them according to student requests. Future changes will not be allowed, so please be sure to get you child's input before selecting!

Please note: enrollment in any band or choral ensemble is a full-year commitment; if you have difficulty or have questions in selecting a music program for your student, please contact Mr. Martinez at mmartinez@kentfieldschools.org (preferred) or at (415) 257-0733, ext. 13 #.
Fifth Grade Music Program
Beginning Band
Beginning band is only offered in fifth grade: it is a prerequisite for any future band classes.

The first year of instrumental music focuses on learning to read music and basic ensemble skills. Students may choose from the following instruments: flute, clarinet, trumpet, trombone or percussion. Students who wish to play the saxophone in the sixth grade band must start on clarinet for the first year (for pedagogical reasons). Students do not need to decide which instrument they want to play until after classes have started. Information about instrument rentals will be sent home with students the first week of school.
Beginning Chorus
Students who choose to participate in beginning chorus will learn musical and vocal techniques through sight singing and unison/part singing. Song selections will range from popular to classical. Emphasis is placed on reading and understanding music notation and how it relates to choral singing.

Both the chorus and band students perform at least twice during the school year.

Required and Optional Sixth Grade Performing Arts Selections
All sixth graders must select a performing arts class (either Intermediate Band or Percussion & Drama) that occurs during the regular school day. In addition, all sixth graders may choose to enroll in Concert Choir, which meets twice a week during zero period at 7:45 A.M. on Tuesday and Thursday.
Sixth Grade Performing Arts: Intermediate Band
The prerequisite for intermediate band is completion of Beginning Band or by audition. The second year of instrumental music improves the musical and ensemble skills learned in Beginning Band and adds multi-part sheet music, syncopation, and major and minor scales.
Sixth Grade Performing Arts: Percussion & Drama
In this program, each student will participate in one semester of Percussion and one semester of Drama. This course is required for all students who enroll in fifth grade Chorus and who have not auditioned for Intermediate Band. Other students may select this course on a space-available basis. The class will be limited to the first 50 students.

The Percussion & Drama students will put on an evening performance at the end of each semester.
Percussion Component - The percussion component of the sixth grade Percussion & Drama class exposes students to the basic fundamentals of percussion technique. In this class, emphasis is placed on reading music notation, learning rehearsal technique, and performing standard percussion ensemble repertoire on various percussion ensemble instruments.
Drama Component - The drama component of the sixth grade Percussion & Drama class explores improvisation, dialogues and multi-character sketches. The class emphasizes actors writing original scripts, selecting an appropriate wardrobe, and performing for their classmates and the community. This course encourages students to "risk big" and discover the magic of being on stage.

Seventh and Eighth Grade Music Program
Optional Zero-Period Advanced Band
The concert band meets during zero period, which begins at 7:45 A.M. on Monday, Wednesday, and Friday of each week. Concert band students must have had two to three years of previous playing experience. Emphasis is placed on gaining musical independence through more extensive scale studies, technical exercises, and improvisation. Students work on music from a wide range of musical styles, including jazz and classical.
Optional Zero-Period Concert Choir
All sixth, seventh and eighth grade students are eligible to participate in concert choir which meets during zero period at 7:45 A.M. on Tuesday and Thursday of each week. Continued emphasis is placed on reading music as well as two, three, and even four part a&cappella as well as accompanied singing.

Both the advanced band and concert choir perform at least twice during the school year.

Eighth Grade Enrichment Classes
Eighth grade students will be enrolled in enrichment classes each trimester. Every eighth-grade student will take a required trimester class of writing workshop; as well, students will be enrolled in two different enrichment classes selected from the following list:
  • Architecture
  • Art
  • Technology
  • Video
  • Wood Shop
Eighth Grade Architecture (Limited Availability)
Design a residence, a 3-D virtual museum on computer software or explore the world of sustainable, green design. In this course, students will design a project that reflects their own interests and then bring it to life. Students will have the choice to use computer software to enhance their design or to build a scale model of their project. Examples of past projects have included a unique sushi restaurant with an interior waterway, a detailed interior design of a residence, a fashion runway, a museum, and a skateboard park. This course will be offered for one trimester this year.
Eighth Grade Art
Eighth grade students will do a series of art projects beginning with a color review and a value study. Other projects that students may work on in this studio include linoleum block carving and printing, sculpture, and a combination painting and performance piece involving art history.
Eighth Grade Technology
Students in this technology lab will produce and maintain Kent's student web pages (www.falconnews.org), and put on the Monday morning Falcon News broadcast. Participants will develop skills in web design: organization of visual, audio, and content elements to attract visitors; and in basic TV journalism: researching, writing, and producing Kent-relevant news stories.
Eighth Grade Video
This video boot camp will expose students to the fundamental elements of visual storytelling: camera, sound, lighting and editing. Students will work in small production crews to complete practice assignments that demonstrate their understanding of each of these elements, and finally will produce a short video project. This is a hands-on class where students will use video cameras, sound equipment, and computer editing programs. Collaboration, creativity, and responsibility are emphasized.
Eighth Grade Wood Shop
From a choice of hardwoods, design and build that heirloom project for yourself or your family; one that will be passed on from generation to generation. Students will learn advanced techniques using woodworking machinery to build their masterpieces. Previous projects have included musical instruments, furniture, skateboards and advanced lathe work; many of these projects have won best of show at the Marin County Fair and ribbons at the California State Fair.
