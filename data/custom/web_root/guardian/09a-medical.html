1. Medication - Daily and 72 hour Supply
In order to administer prescription or over-the-counter medication at school, the District requires a medication form, signed by a doctor. The forms for daily medication ("Authorization to Administer Medication" / "Autorizaci√≥n de administrar medicaciones") and for 72 hour emergency medication ("72 Hour Disaster Medications" / "Medicaciones del desastre de 72 horas") can be downloaded from from the Marin County Office of Education School Health Manual page, or picked up in one of the school offices.

Please complete all appropriate form(s) and return them to the school office with the necessary medication. You must bring the required medication in the original container, labeled with the student's name, on the first day of school or as soon as new medication is dispensed. Full school participation, safety and wellness are dependent on complete information along with medication as needed. If you need additional help, please contact the District nurse, Becky Gayman, at bgayman@kentfieldschools.org
